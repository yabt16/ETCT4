Cadet Training Programs

Structured pathways from first sea contract to senior officer rank — covering pre-sea preparation, post-sea advancement, and everything in between.

Pre-Sea Training Programme

Designed for aspiring marine engineers with no prior sea experience. This programme builds the foundational knowledge, safety awareness, and practical skills required before boarding a vessel for the first time. Completing pre-sea training is a mandatory step toward your first contract.

Course Modules

What You Will Learn

Module 01

Basic Safety Training (STCW VI/1)

Personal survival techniques, fire prevention and fire fighting, elementary first aid, and personal safety and social responsibilities.

⏱ 4 weeks
Module 02

Marine Engineering Fundamentals

Introduction to diesel engines, boilers, pumps, piping systems, and basic ship structure for engine department cadets.

⏱ 6 weeks
Module 03

Engine Room Watchkeeping

Principles of watchkeeping at sea, standing orders, logbook keeping, and communication between engine and bridge departments.

⏱ 3 weeks
Module 04

Ship Stability & Buoyancy

Basic naval architecture, stability principles, load line regulations, and hull inspection essentials for engine officers.

⏱ 2 weeks
Module 05

Maritime Law & Regulations

SOLAS, MARPOL, MLC 2006 seafarer rights, flag state regulations, and port state control procedures.

⏱ 2 weeks
Module 06

Engine Room Simulator Practice

Hands-on simulator training covering normal operations, emergency scenarios, and fault-finding in a controlled environment.

⏱ 3 weeks

Post-Sea Training & Upgrading

For engineers already serving at sea who want to upgrade their COC, attain new endorsements, or prepare for management-level responsibilities. Post-sea training is typically completed during your shore leave period and feeds directly into higher rank certification.

Upgrade Pathways

Post-Sea Programmes

Upgrade 01

Officer of the Watch → 3rd Engineer

Transition from watchkeeping duties to independent operation. Covers advanced machinery systems, fault diagnosis, and management basics.

⏱ 8 weeks
Upgrade 02

3rd Engineer → 2nd Engineer

Advanced maintenance planning, crew supervision, technical reporting, and preparation for STCW III/1 advanced endorsement exam.

⏱ 10 weeks
Upgrade 03

2nd Engineer → Chief Engineer

Management Level Certificate (STCW III/2) preparation. Leadership, ERM, technical management, and Chief Engineer exam coaching.

⏱ 16 weeks
Upgrade 04

Engine Room Resource Management (ERM)

Mandatory for management level officers. Team leadership, decision-making, workload management, and effective communication at sea.

⏱ 5 days
Upgrade 05

Tanker & Gas Carrier Specialist

Endorsements for working on oil tankers, chemical tankers, and gas carriers per STCW VI/1, V/1-1, and V/1-2 requirements.

⏱ 2–4 weeks
Upgrade 06

GMDSS & Electronic Navigation

General Operator Certificate for Global Maritime Distress and Safety System, AIS operations, and bridge communication protocols.

⏱ 3 weeks

Step by Step

How to Apply for Training

Follow these steps to enroll in a training programme through ECA.

1

Create an ECA Membership Account

Register on the ECA website as a cadet member. Membership is free and gives you access to all training applications, scholarships, and the job board.

2

Complete Your Profile

Fill in your educational background, sea service record (if any), and upload your identification documents and academic certificates.

3

Choose Your Programme

Browse the Pre-Sea or Post-Sea training options, select the programme that matches your current level, and check intake dates and availability.

4

Submit Application & Documents

Submit your programme application with all required supporting documents. ECA will review and confirm eligibility within 5–7 working days.

5

Receive Placement Confirmation

Once approved, you will receive a placement letter from ECA and the partner institution, including start dates and reporting instructions.

6

Start Training & Track Progress

Log your progress in the ECA Career Tracker on the Financial Support page. ECA mentors are available throughout your training for guidance.
